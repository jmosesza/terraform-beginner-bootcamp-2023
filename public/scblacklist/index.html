Aim



  • In Blacklist, players take on the role of Sam Fisher, the main character of the series, as he attempts to put an end to the Engineers, a terrorist organization.


  • The third-person viewpoint is used throughout gameplay, with an emphasis on stealth.


  • The Paladin, the head quarters of the 4th Echelon, has a sizable table known as the Strategic Mission Interface, or SMI for short.


  • You can view your available 4th Echelon and Campaign missions by interacting with it.

Missions



  • To gain in-game currency and unlock more weapons or gadgets, complete these missions.


  • Playing as Sam Fisher, you embark on 13 different single-player quests.


  • During the campaign, 14 more cooperative missions are accessible.


  • You can play the majority of these missions solo or in split screen, but you have to play every Briggs Mission with a CO-OP Partner.

Difficulty Settings



  • Rookie: For gamers who are unfamiliar with the genre.


  • Normal: Suggested for gamers with experience.


  • Realistic: Customized for seasoned Splinter Cell users.


  • Perfectionist: For those who are proficient in Realistic gaming.

Earnings and Spending



  • Earning and Spending


  • You can spend the money you earn from each Campaign or 4th Echelon Mission match in any of the game modes.


  • You can spend money to:


  • Customize Gear: Unlocking and upgrading new gear, including goggles, ops suits, and weapons.


  • Upgrade Paladin: Among the many advantages of upgrading Fourth Echelon's Headquarters is an increase in your earnings rate.

Gear



  • All of weapons and gadgets in Sam Fisher's arsenal falls under the Gear section.


  • The gear can be found and customized in the pause menu or at the Workshop in the HQ.


  • Only select items will be unlocked from the start of the game.


  • Other items must be purchased from the HQ or black market dealers.


  • Some pieces of gear must be unlocked through Campaign and 4th Echelon Missions.


  • Gadgets: These are the small tools that are used for recon, distraction, disabling, and killing enemies.


  • Goggles: These are the various optics that are used to see at night, through walls, and more.


  • Ops Suit: The suit is the last line of defense for Sam. They will vary with stealth, armor, and more.


  • Pistols: The various handguns in Sam's arsenal.


  • Alternative Weapons: The various rifles, machine guns, and shotguns in Sam's arsenal.


  • Special Weapons: These are the weapons that don't really fall into any category.

Paladin Upgrades



  • Paladin is the name of the airplane that serves as the 4th Echelon Headquarters.


  • It's one of many things that can be upgraded with the money earned throughout the game. Talk to Grim to select upgrades.


  • Upgrading the Paladin will give you a 4E Multiplier. This will net you more money for every point you score.

Stealth Tactics



  • Splinter Cell is usually attributed with Stealth gameplay.


  • If you want to play like a Ghost, you may need a quick crash course in Stealth Tactics.


  • Lights & Shadow


    • The basics of the basics is hiding in shadows to avoid detection.
    • While hidden, your Ops Suits Lights will flare to notify you.
    • Crouching will also reduce your visibility.
    • Use caution in Daylight Missions or near enemies with Flashlights.
    • Although shadows help, hostiles can still spot you if they get close.
    • The higher the difficulty, the easier it is for them to spot you.


  • Hand to Hand


    • Not all fighting in Splinter Cell: Blacklist is done with firearms.
    • Sometimes you need to get up close and personal with Hand to Hand combat.


  • Basics


    • Enemies can either be knocked out or killed with simple Hand to Hand, preferably while still in cover, near a hostile to take them down.
    • You can switch between Lethal or Non-Lethal takedown techniques.
    • Enemies can also be taken and used as a Human Shield for protection.


  • Abductions




    • If you're trying to remain stealthy, it's best to abduct enemies who are on patrol.
    • While in cover, wait for the target to come by or call them.
    • When they are near, perform an abduction, dragging the target out of sight.
    • This will help you remain undetected.


  • Advanced


    • Some hostiles will rebuff attempts to take them down from the front.
    • Instead, try attacking from unique positions.


    • When hanging from a ledge or pipe to perform a Death from Above (or Aerial Knock Out).
    • While hanging from a ledge, pull a hostile over the edge or knock them out against the wall.


  • Mark and Execute


    • Splinter Cell: Blacklist allows you to perform smooth executions with the Mark and Execute system.


    • The system allows all players to flow through enemies like a true elite operative.


    • Make Note: On Realistic Difficulty, the Execute Gauge fills slowly.


    • You cannot Execute at all on Perfectionist.
